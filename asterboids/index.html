Aster-boids

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    Yarr! features an AI opponent and a pirate-themed strategy game where the location of the opponent's cannons are unknown.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    I made a turn-based system that loops and has AI interactions. I used tween animations for fancy effects, and the ship grid system is a single array in which elements store an internal row and column, to locate where they are relative to other pieces.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    I learned how to use phaser's timers to make events happen in sequence, and I used tween.onComplete to do the same but with associated animations.
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    The hardest part was making the opponent aim at the player. I had to chain tweens, but the number of chained tweens was variable as it could be anywhere from 0-4. Instead of doing many if statements I wanted to call the onComplete function of the tween to start the next tween and also to kill the target square. The problem with this was the scope of the tween didn't include the sprite the tween was aiming at, so I had to do some fancy editing to give the tween access to that. I also did eventually find out how to have an indeterminate amount of tweens chained together via for loop.

  • Gameplay: A paragraph describing the actions the player can perform, the system dynamics, and the core mechanic. Include a concise explanation of the prototypeâ€™s inputs and their expected effects (how to play). You can also describe game play that is not in the prototype. You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    The only controls the player has are mouse inputs. There are two clickable zones on the screen, which are your ship and your enemy's ship. The prompts at the top of the screen tell you what phase of the battle you're in, with green text meaning your turn, and red text meaning the enemy's turn. Play starts with selecting the deck of the ship that will be used in the attack, and the cannons on that floor contribute to the number of hits you get. After both the player and the computer attack, the game enters the repair state, in which one single room can be repaired. Cannons in disabled rooms are not counted in the total number of attacks a deck has. After the repair phase, the attack phase begins again. The objective is to destroy two decks of the player's ship.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    The computer always chooses the floor with the highest cannon count. Attacking that floor increases the chances of disabling the enemy cannons, which can turn the game into a win!
