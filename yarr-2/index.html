Yarr! (remake)

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    Yarr! Idle has a complex ship-building mechanic in which you can expand your arsenal and your ship's size!
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    I had to design a way that the squares of the ship would detect empty spaces around them (with a total size limit in mind) and that you could build new boxes in these spaces. The structure used is a dual-axis "quadruply-linked list" that I implemented.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    I learned how to create and represent complex data structures in Javascript.
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    The most difficult part was getting the room placement just right. I ran into many bugs just in an attempt to get rooms to sit correctly and be placed and removed correctly. I also didn't want the placement tiles to be overlapping, since when you're placing something and it borders two other squares, it still only takes up one square itself, and having stacked sprites with alpha looked ugly anyway. I fixed it by doing a once-over loop of the areas and checking for doubles.

    The only controls the player has are mouse inputs. Along the top of the screen is the current treasure amount, as well as a list of things to buy. At the moment, only the first two items have any impact on gameplay, as they grant the players more rooms and more weapons, respectively. Clicking and dragging on the icons begins the placement popup, which shows where items can be placed. Placing them successfully and with sufficient funds results in a purchase. Money is gained by destroying rooms on enemy ships, and each room of your own ship can only hold a maximum of three items (at this point, it is advisable to only use cannons).
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    Buy more cannons as soon as you have the money. Fill up every room with 3 and your attack power will be extremely high.
