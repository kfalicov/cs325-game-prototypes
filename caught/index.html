Butterfly Catch

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    The game is a puzzle game that involves dividing up butterflies and trapping them.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    This game has BMP generated graphics for the "crosshair" as well as a constantly expanding list of wall colliders and bounce physics.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    I utilized the debug mode as a feature for drawing. I also learned how to resize the game window while the game is still in play (still buggy at the moment)
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    It was difficult to figure out how to implement the trap mechanism, at first I wanted to create rectangles and sprites, but it ended up with all of this complicated parenting stuff so I just let the debug mode handle rendering, and it ended up being much easier.

You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    The mouse is the only control. Moving the mouse moves either a vertical or horizontal axis, and clicking will place a "wall" along that axis. After a click, the axis switches from horizontal to vertical or vice versa. The goal is to trap butterflies in their matching color squares in the field. Eventually I want to implement scoring or a way to complete levels, this will be done by starting a timer when a butterfly goes over the correct square, and if a certain time elapses it can be considered caught. I also have to make more precise checks on initial velocity so that the butterfly can't be moving perfectly vertically or horizontally. Lastly, I might make the wall generation more random than the two axes, and include diagonals or rotation.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    My first playtester informed me that you can resize the window to push butterflies around. Also, if a butterfly is completely trapped you can inch it slowly out by putting walls against it, which push the butterfly past other walls. Aside from exploits, dividing up the butterflies and patiently assessing the situation before making moves will help.
