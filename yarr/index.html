  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    The game is a fun little physics sim with launch functionality and collisions
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    The game is now a solid engine and can be easily expanded in the future. You can hit things in the world and launch them, and then go and hit them again. It's close to being a real golf game at this point.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    I fixed the resize feature so the game displays at variable sizes (with a max size). I also figured out how to detect intersection with a circle, which I used for the power gauge during launch. I also used atlases for animation.
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    The hardest part was making the swings apply to the nearest ball within a range. At first, I had it set to spawn balls when you clicked, but it's much more fun to use the environment objects. The easiest part was learning how to draw circles at a specific location.

    A and D for movement, W or Spacebar for jump, and mouse click and drag to hit a ball. The balls cannot be hit unless you are close enough to hit them with the golf club.     The Golfer is an average man with an affinity for golf. He gets turned down by the Ritz hotel when trying to get a room, but determined to gain access to the fancy hotel, he decides to sneak in the roof. As a golfer his skill set involves hitting random objects with his golf club, with the objective of distracting or knocking out the guards on the roof.
